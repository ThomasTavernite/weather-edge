📖 How to Read This Dashboard

1

We pull forecasts from Open-Meteo

Every few minutes, WeatherBid checks Open-Meteo — an open-source service aggregating multiple global weather models (GFS, ECMWF, and more) — for high/low temperature forecasts for each city.

2

We compare forecasts against Kalshi's market prices

Kalshi lists temperature markets with brackets like "72° to 73°." Each bracket has a price — buy "Yes" on a bracket you believe in, and if you're right, each contract pays $1.00. For example, if you spot an underpriced bracket at 10¢ and buy 100 contracts for $10, and it hits — you get $100 back. That's a $90 profit from a $10 position. Scale it up: $100 in could become $1,000 out.

3

The "← Forecast" tag shows where forecasts point

In the bracket table, the row tagged ← Forecast is the bracket the forecast temperature falls into.

4

Signals tell you if the market agrees or disagrees

✓ ALIGNED — Forecast and the market's top bracket match. No edge.

⚡ DIVERGENCE — Forecast points to a different bracket than the market favorite. Potential opportunity — someone is wrong.
5

Divergence ≠ guaranteed profit

Weather models can be wrong. Kalshi settles on the official NWS Daily Climate Report — not forecasts. A divergence means "worth investigating," not "free money." Never trade more than you can afford to lose.
