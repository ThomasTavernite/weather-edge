📖 How this dashboard works

1

~143 ensemble members, not 3 deterministic runs

We pull the GEFS (31), ECMWF ENS (51), ICON-EPS (40), and GEM (21) ensemble systems from Open-Meteo — roughly 143 possible futures per city. We don't average them into one number; we use the whole distribution.

2

Probability per bracket, directly

For each Kalshi bracket, we count how many ensemble members land there. If 42 of 143 members say the high will be 72–73°F, that bracket has a 29% probability. Simple, honest math — no hand-tuned weights.

3

EV = our probability ÷ market price − 1

If our probability is 29% and Kalshi is pricing the bracket at 20¢, EV = +45%. Positive EV means the bracket is underpriced vs. our model. The opposite is also true — we'll show you brackets the market is overvaluing.

4

Bias correction + live observation override

Every time Kalshi settles a market, we record the error between our ensemble mean and the actual high. That bias gets subtracted from future forecasts for that city. After 1 PM local, if the observed temp is already higher than any member predicted, we clip the distribution upward.

5

Signal types

🎯 OPPORTUNITY — Best-EV bracket has ≥15% expected edge, ≥8% probability, and market has real volume.
👁 WATCH — Positive EV but below the OPPORTUNITY threshold.
⚖ ALIGNED — Our top-probability bracket matches the market's favorite. No edge.
NO_EDGE / LOW_DATA / LOW_VOLUME — Distribution doesn't suggest a tradeable divergence.
6

This is not financial advice

Ensemble forecasts are not oracles. Brackets can and do settle at $0. Never risk what you can't lose. Track record below is raw — no cherry-picking.
